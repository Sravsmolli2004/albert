ALBERT EINSTEIN

    
A Brief Biography of ALBERT EINSTEIN
 


     

**Early Life and Education**


Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm,,in the Kingdom of WÃ¼rttemberg in the German Empire.
 
He grew up in a secular Jewish family and showed an early interest in science and mathematics.

 In 1896, he enrolled in the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich, where he earned his diploma in 1900.


**Early Career and Annus Mirabilis**


After graduation, Einstein worked at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern, which gave him the time to conduct research.

 In 1905, often referred to as his "annus mirabilis" or "miracle year", Einstein published four groundbreaking papers.

  These papers covered the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and mass-energy equivalence   (E=mc^2).
 
  These works laid the foundation for modern physics and established Einstein as a leading physicist.



**Academic Career and General Relativity**


In 1915, Einstein completed his theory of general relativity, which revolutionized the understanding of gravity.
 
This theory was confirmed in 1919 when observations during a solar eclipse showed light bending around the sun, consistent with Einstein's predictions. 

Einstein became an international celebrity and a symbol of scientific genius.



**Later Years and Legacy**


Einstein emigrated to the United States in 1933, escaping the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany.
 
He took a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey, where he remained until his death.
 
Although he contributed to the development of quantum theory, Einstein famously disagreed with the interpretation of quantum mechanics, encapsulated in his statement, "God does not play dice with the universe."

Albert Einstein passed away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton. His contributions to physics, including his theories of relativity, have had a profound and lasting impact on the field.

 Einstein's name has become synonymous with genius, and his legacy continues to influence scientific thought and popular culture.



**Notable Achievements:**


 **1905**: Annus Mirabilis papers, including E=mc^2.

 **1915**: General Theory of Relativity.

 **1921**: Nobel Prize in Physics for the photoelectric effect.

 **1933**: Emigration to the United States.

 **1955**: Death in Princeton, New Jersey.


Einstein's work transcended the realm of physics, touching on philosophy, politics, and humanitarian efforts, solidifying his place as one of the most influential figures of the 20th century.
